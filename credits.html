PROGRAM INFO
HISTORY

The CMDC Program was founded in 1997 and intended to be an interdisciplinary program that looked critically and responded creatively to issues relating digital media. The name was changed to Digital Technology and Culture (DTC) in 2003, when the program was approved to offer the B.A. in DTC.
HISTORY

The CMDC Program was founded in 1997 and intended to be an interdisciplinary program that looked critically and responded creatively to issues relating digital media. The name was changed to Digital Technology and Culture (DTC) in 2003, when the program was approved to offer the B.A. in DTC.
ABOUT US

The Creative Media & Digital Culture Program offers a major and a formal minor leading to the Bachelor of Arts Degree (B.A.) in Digital Technology and Culture (DTC). Additionally, students pursuing a B.A. in the Humanities or a B.A. in Social Sciences can select the DTC as a primary or secondary area of concentration. In all cases, The Creative Media & Digital Culture Program teaches students to conceptualize, in both research and practice, applications of digital technologies and to think critically about digital media and the ways humans interact and engage with them.

AREAS OF STUDY

The program focuses on six areas of study within the field of digital media:
  • Web & Mobile Design & development
  • 2D & 3D Animation for Simulation and Visualization
  • Digital Publishing
  • Physical Computing
  • Social Media / SEO for Digital Marketing
  • Game Studies & Design
